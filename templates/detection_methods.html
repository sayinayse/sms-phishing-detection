Rule-Based SMS Phishing Detection

Detection Rules

Suspicious Links

The system checks for URLs that could lead to phishing sites.

Example: "Click here to win a prize!"

Suspicious Keywords

Certain words or phrases often used in phishing messages trigger a warning.

Example: "Congratulations! You've won a prize!"

Time Pressure

Messages creating urgency (e.g., "Last chance!" or "Your account will be closed soon") are flagged.

Example: "You must act now!"

Fake Authority Claims

If the message pretends to be from a trusted authority (e.g., tax office or police), it's considered phishing.

Example: "Your account has been suspended due to an unpaid tax."

Unusual Formatting

Excessive capitalization, multiple exclamation marks, or ellipses are signs of phishing attempts.

Example: "URGENT!!! YOUR ACCOUNT IS BLOCKED!!!"

Foreign Numbers

SMS messages originating from foreign numbers (not from a local phone number) are flagged as suspicious.

Example: "+44 20 7946 0958"

Fake Login Links

The system checks for links resembling login pages but leading to fake websites.

Example: "log-in to your bank account at www.ziraaatbank.com."

Generic Greetings

Phishing messages often use vague greetings like "Dear customer" or "Valued user."

Example: "Dear customer, your account has been compromised."

How It Works

  • Step 1: Each incoming SMS is processed by the is_phishing_sms function.
  • Step 2: The message is checked against the predefined rules.
  • Step 3: If any rule is violated, the message is flagged as phishing.
  • Step 4: The system also provides the specific rule violated for transparency.
