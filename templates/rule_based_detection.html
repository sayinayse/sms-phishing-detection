Rule-Based SMS Phishing Detection

How It Works

  • Step 1: Each incoming SMS is processed by the is_phishing_sms function.
  • Step 2: The message is checked against the predefined rules.
  • Step 3: If any rule is violated, the message is flagged as phishing.
  • Step 4: The system also provides the specific rule violated for transparency.

Detection Rules

  • Suspicious Links: The system checks for URLs that could lead to phishing sites.
    Example: "Click here to win a prize!", "Kazandınız", "Promosyon kazandınız"
  • Suspicious Keywords: Certain words or phrases often used in phishing messages trigger a warning.
    Example: "Congratulations! You've won a prize!", "Tebrikler ödül kazandınız"
  • Time Pressure: Messages creating urgency (e.g., "Last chance!" or "Your account will be closed soon") are flagged.
    Example: "You must act now!", "Bugün içinde işlem yapın", "Son gün", "Hemen tıkla"
  • Fake Authority Claims: If the message pretends to be from a trusted authority (e.g., tax office or police), it's considered phishing.
    Example: "You have an unpaid tax.", "Hesabınız kapatılacak", "Adınıza açılmış bir dava var", "Polis tarafından aranıyorsunuz"
  • Unusual Formatting: Excessive capitalization, multiple exclamation marks, or ellipses are signs of phishing attempts.
    Example: "URGENT!!! YOUR ACCOUNT IS BLOCKED!!!", "Hemen tıkla!!!!", "BUGÜN SON ŞANS"
  • Foreign Numbers: SMS messages originating from foreign numbers (not from a local phone number) are flagged as suspicious.
    Example: "+44 20 7946 0958"
  • Fake Login Links: The system checks for links resembling login pages but leading to fake websites.
    Example: "log-in to your bank account at www.ziraaatbank.com."
  • Generic Greetings: Phishing messages often use vague greetings like "Dear customer" or "Valued user."
    Example: "Dear customer, your account has been compromised.", "Sayın müşteri", "Değerli müşteri"
